Everything About VAT in France

Value Added Tax (VAT) is an indirect tax paid by consumers and collected by businesses. Calculating VAT is essential for freelancers, merchants, and even individuals who want to know the real price excluding tax of a product. In France, VAT represents about 45% of state tax revenue, or more than 200 billion euros per year.

Whether you're an entrepreneur, accountant, or simple consumer, understanding the VAT mechanism allows you to better manage your finances and avoid invoicing errors.

VAT Rates in Effect (2025)

  • 20% (Standard Rate): Applies to most goods and services (clothing, electronics, services, etc.).
  • 10% (Intermediate Rate): Restaurants, transport, renovation work, certain accommodations.
  • 5.5% (Reduced Rate): Essential products, books, energy (gas/electricity), show tickets.
  • 2.1% (Super-Reduced Rate): Reimbursed medications, press.

Note: certain French territories (DOM-TOM, Corsica) apply specific rates. Corsica benefits from a standard rate of 20% but a reduced rate of 10% for certain products.

Who Must Declare VAT?

All businesses subject to VAT must collect it and remit it to the state. Micro-entrepreneurs benefit from a VAT exemption up to certain thresholds: â‚¬91,900 for sale of goods and â‚¬36,800 for service provision. Beyond that, the business must charge VAT and declare it monthly or quarterly.

VAT FAQ

Who pays VAT?

It's the final consumer who pays VAT. The business only collects it to remit it to the state. Businesses subject to VAT can recover VAT on their professional purchases.

What's the difference between Excl. VAT and Incl. VAT?

Excl. VAT (Excluding Tax) = price without VAT. Incl. VAT (Including Tax) = final price paid by the customer, including VAT. For a product at â‚¬100 Excl. VAT with 20% VAT, the Incl. VAT price is â‚¬120.

Can you recover VAT on all purchases?

No. Certain expenses don't allow recovery: passenger vehicles (except utility vehicles), fuel (partially recoverable), restaurant and accommodation expenses (limited). Purchases must be justified by a compliant invoice.

What happens if I exceed the exemption threshold?

You must charge VAT from the 1st day of the month of exceeding. You'll also need to register for VAT with the SIE (Business Tax Service) and declare collected VAT monthly or quarterly.

How do I know which VAT rate to apply?

The rate depends on the nature of the product or service sold. If in doubt, consult the official list on impots.gouv.fr or contact your accountant. Applying the wrong rate can lead to a tax audit.
