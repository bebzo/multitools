What is ROI (Return on Investment)?

ROI (Return On Investment) is the number one financial indicator for measuring investment effectiveness. It tells you how much you gained (or lost) relative to the initially invested amount. Whether for real estate, stock market, advertising, or entrepreneurial investment, ROI is the universal profitability indicator.

A good ROI varies by sector: in the stock market, 7-8% annually is excellent. In e-commerce (Facebook/Google Ads advertising), we often aim for a 300% ROI (i.e., x3) to cover all ancillary costs (logistics, customer service, etc.).

The ROI Formula

The calculation is universal:

ROI (%) = ((Final Gain - Amount Invested) / Amount Invested) x 100

How to Interpret the Result?

  • Positive ROI (> 0%): Your operation is profitable. For â‚¬100 invested, you recovered your stake + a profit.
  • Negative ROI (< 0%): You lost money. The recovered amount is less than the invested amount.

Finance FAQ

What is a good ROI?

It depends on the sector. Real estate: 5-10% gross. Stock market: 7-8% annually. Online advertising: 200-500%. The higher the ROI, the more profitable the investment.

How to calculate ROI for a real estate investment?

Gross ROI = (Annual Rent / Purchase Price) x 100. For net ROI, deduct expenses (property tax, work, vacancy, management fees). A good gross rental ROI is between 5% and 10% depending on the city.

What's the difference between ROI and profitability?

ROI measures return relative to initial investment (in %). Profitability can designate several things: profit margin, rate of return, etc. ROI is more precise and universal.

What does a 100% ROI mean?

It means you doubled your stake. For â‚¬1,000 invested, you recovered â‚¬2,000 (â‚¬1,000 capital + â‚¬1,000 gain). That's an excellent result!

Does ROI account for time?

No, simple ROI doesn't account for duration. A 50% ROI in 1 year is much better than a 50% ROI in 10 years. To compare, use annualized ROI.
