Tips for a Secure Password

The security of your online accounts depends almost exclusively on the strength of your passwords. A good password must be impossible to guess for a human and very long to crack for a computer. In 2024, 81% of data breaches are due to weak or reused passwords (source: Verizon DBIR).

With the rise of artificial intelligence and brute force attacks, an 8-character password can be cracked in a few hours. That's why we recommend a minimum of 16 characters with a mix of types.

ANSSI Golden Rules

  • Length: 12 characters minimum (ideally 16+).
  • Complexity: Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters.
  • Uniqueness: NEVER use the same password for two different sites (especially for your email).
  • No dictionary words: Avoid common words, names, dates of birth.

Cracking Time by Length

Here's the estimated time to crack a random password with all character types (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols):

  • 8 characters: A few hours (DANGEROUS)
  • 12 characters: About 200 years (Acceptable)
  • 16 characters: Several billion years (Excellent)

These estimates assume a brute force attack with a powerful computer. Passwords based on dictionary words ("password123", "qwerty", first names) can be cracked instantly.

Security FAQ

Is it safe to use this generator?

Yes. Generation is 100% in your browser (Client-side). The password is never sent over the internet.

Should I use a password manager?

YES, absolutely! A manager like Bitwarden, 1Password, or Dashlane allows you to generate and store unique passwords for each site. You only need to remember one master password.

Should I regularly change my passwords?

No, except in case of data breach. ANSSI recommends NOT regularly changing a strong password, as this pushes users to choose weaker passwords or reuse them.

What if a site limits password length?

If a site limits to 12-16 characters, use the maximum allowed with all character types. If the limit is less than 12, it's a bad sign of the site's security.

Are passwords with phrases secure?

Yes! "Passphrases" like "Coffee!Sun#2025@Paris" are excellent: long, easy to remember, and difficult to crack. Mix words, numbers, and symbols.
