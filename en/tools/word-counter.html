Why Use an Online Word Counter?

Whether you're a web writer, student, journalist, or community manager, respecting a character or word limit is often crucial. This free and instant counter allows you to check the length of your texts without installing anything. Over 2 million writers use word counters every day to optimize their content.

Whether it's to respect an editorial constraint, optimize your SEO, or simply check the length of a tweet, this tool is essential in your digital toolbox.

Common Limits (Social Media & SEO)

  • Twitter (X): 280 characters max.
  • Meta Description (SEO): Between 150 and 160 characters recommended.
  • SEO Blog Article: Google often favors in-depth articles of over 1500 words.
  • LinkedIn (Post): Up to 3000 characters.

Difference Between "Words" and "Characters"

The number of characters includes all letters, numbers, punctuation, and spaces. The number of words counts groups of characters separated by spaces. Note: some word processors count apostrophes differently.

Common Use Cases

  • SEO Web Writing: Google favors articles of 1500-2500 words for competitive queries.
  • Theses and Dissertations: Respect the imposed word count (often 10,000 to 50,000 words).
  • Google Ads Advertising: Title limited to 30 characters, description to 90 characters.
  • CV and Cover Letters: Ideally 200-400 words for a cover letter.

Writing FAQ

Does this counter include spaces?

Yes, in the "Characters" count, spaces are included. This is the standard for social networks and most writing tools.

What's the ideal length for a blog article?

For SEO, aim for 1500-2500 words for competitive topics. For news articles or simple tutorials, 800-1200 words are sufficient. Quality always trumps quantity.

How to count words in a PDF?

Copy the text from the PDF and paste it into our tool. Note: some scanned PDFs (images) don't allow text copying. Use an OCR tool in that case.

Do spaces count as characters?

Yes, in our "Characters" counter, spaces are included. This is the norm for social networks and most writing tools.

How many words for a 5-minute speech?

On average, we speak at 130-150 words per minute. For a 5-minute speech, aim for 650-750 words. Practice aloud to adjust.
