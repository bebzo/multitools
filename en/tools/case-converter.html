Case Converter

Transform text case instantly.

Why Use a Case Converter?

It often happens that you need to change the format of an entire text: having written in CAPS LOCK by mistake (Caps Lock key stuck), wanting to properly format titles, or adapt text for a specific design. Over 500,000 users per month use case converters to quickly correct their texts.

Whether it's to respect a style guide, correct a massive typo, or simply test different styles, this tool saves you precious time.

Available Formats

  • UPPERCASE: Transforms all letters to capitals (UPPERCASE). Useful for titles or warnings.
  • lowercase: Transforms everything to small letters (lowercase).
  • Title Case: Capitalizes The First Letter Of Each Word. Often used in English titles (Title Case).
  • aLtErNaTe: The "Spongebob Case" style, often used ironically on the internet.

Common Use Cases

  • Error Correction: You wrote an entire paragraph in CAPS LOCK by mistake.
  • Blog/Video Titles: Properly format your titles in Title Case.
  • Data Normalization: Standardize lists of names (e.g., customer databases).
  • Design and Mockups: Quickly test different typographic styles.

Formatting FAQ

Is my data saved?

No, conversion happens instantly in your browser via Javascript. No text is sent to our servers.

What's the difference between Title Case and Sentence case?

Title Case capitalizes each word (Example Of Title). Sentence case only capitalizes the first letter of the sentence (Example of title). In English, Title Case is more common for headings.
